Comparative Analysis of Pathfinding Algorithms

In a dynamic collaboration, our team embarked on an extensive analysis of classical pathfinding algorithms, including Dijkstra's, Bellman-Ford's, and A*. We delved into intricate graph scenarios encompassing up to 1,000 nodes to assess and compare the performance of each algorithm across a spectrum of network scales and complexities. Our project highlighted the proficiency of Dijkstra's algorithm in standard mapping contexts and unveiled the superior capabilities of the A* algorithm within densely woven, multifaceted networks. A noteworthy application was the simulation of the London subway system, where our optimization strategies propelled computational efficiency forward by over 30%. This project not only showcased the nuanced strengths of each algorithm but also advanced our understanding of their practical implementation in real-world navigation systems.
